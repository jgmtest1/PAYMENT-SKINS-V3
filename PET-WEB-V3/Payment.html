Pet insurance helps you to meet the cost of unexpected vets bills if your pet becomes unwell or has an accident and needs treatment.

Having pet insurance can also help with other costs such as paying for your pet to be looked after in an emergency or the cost of finding your pet if it goes missing or gets stolen.

There are four types of pet insurance:

  • Accident only - just covers accidents and injuries
  • Time limited - covers 12 months for a condition
  • Maximum benefit -  covers a condition up to a maximum vet fee limit (with no time limit)
  • Lifetime - covers ongoing conditions up to a vet fee limit that starts again when you renew your policy

You can buy lifetime pet insurance with us.

We offer lifetime pet insurance for cats and dogs at any age. You can tailor your policy to your pet and its health conditions. 

This means that you could cover ongoing conditions as well as one-off mishaps with no limit to the length of the treatment as long as you renew your policy and there’s no break in cover.

Lifetime pet insurance can cover your pet for both one off and ongoing health conditions as well as accidents or injury. 

The amount of vet fee cover you chose starts again each year as long as there is no break in cover and you renew your policy. This means it can cover long term conditions for the life of your pet.

When setting up your policy, you can choose your excess payment from £0 to £500. By increasing the amount you’ll agree to pay, we’ll decrease the cost of your cover. You’ll be asked to cover your excess once per condition per year. So if your pet has a recurring condition that’s covered you’ll only have to pay the excess once for the entire year.

You’ll also be given the choice of a bill share which would be on top of your excess.

Bill share is often called a ‘co-payment’ by other insurers and is also often  compulsory once your pet reaches either the age of 8 for dogs or 10 for cats. 

With us, it’s optional. We think it’s fairer to let you decide whether you want to foot more of the bill or not. You can even choose your own level from 0% to 30%.

With bill share, you agree to pay a percentage of the claim after your excess has been taken off. We’ll pay the rest up to your cover limits. The higher bill share percentage you choose to pay, the lower your premium will be.

When you’re buying lifetime pet insurance from us, you’ll need to let us know about any of your pet’s pre-existing medical conditions.

This means you’ll need to declare any conditions that they have been diagnosed or treated for in the past 2 years - not including routine flea and worming treatments and vaccinations. Even if they said it wasn’t a big deal, it’s worth telling us about.

Only one person over 18 can be the policyholder - even if your pets have more than one owner. This is the person who will be named on the policy and who will be able to change policy details. 

If you need to nominate someone else to speak on your behalf to make changes or speak about any claims, then you’ll be able to do this by letting customer services know.

Continuous cover means that you won’t be left uncovered when switching from another pet insurance provider. Usually, you’ll have to wait 14 days before you can make a claim if you were starting a new policy. 

Cover must be the same as your existing policy for it to be classed as continuous cover, for example, if your previous policy provided cover for vet fees at the time of your switch, but not for Trip Cancellation, you will have Continuous Cover for vet fees but not for Trip Cancellation.

We don’t normally cover routine or investigative tests. The only time we could cover these is when they’re used to diagnose a new condition or the worsening of an existing condition covered under your policy.

We also won’t cover any routine and preventative treatments, vaccinations, not limited to,  cosmetic dentistry, cosmetic surgery, cleaning and descaling of teeth, spaying, castration, routine removal of dew claws, parasite control, grooming and nail clipping or any complications arising from these treatments.

We can’t cover any treatment related to pregnancy, giving birth or breeding and any related complications.

No, we can’t cover any pets used for breeding.

The only working animals we can cover are Assistance Dogs for personal use.

The last thing you want to be worrying about when you’re hospitalised or seriously ill is whether your pet can be looked after. 

If you’re hospitalised, you’ll be covered for the cost of your pet staying in a kennel or cattery or having a pet minder up to your policy limits.

If you’re unable to walk your dog due to being incapacitated, we’ll cover the cost for a professional dog-walker to walk your dog twice a day up to your policy limits.

Nobody ever wants to think about what’s going to happen when their pet dies, but we can be with you every step of the way when the time comes. All you need to do is add our optional Farewell cover to your policy.

As long as the cause of death is covered by your policy, we’ll cover up to £250 towards the cost of a cremation, burial including the cost of an urn/casket or box.  We will also cover up to £5,000 for the cost of your pet. 

If you buy the optional Travel and Holiday cover, you’ll be covered for vet fees abroad if you decide to travel with your pet worldwide for up to 183 days per year - as long as no trip is longer than 90 days.

With optional Travel and Holiday cover, you’re covered to cancel your trip if:

  • Your pet is lost, stolen or strays
  • Dies, or is put to sleep by a vet as a result of an accidental injury or illness
  • Needs urgent life-saving treatment because of an accidental injury or illness within 7 days of the date you’re due to travel.

You’ll be covered to claim up to your policy limits for:

  • Vet fees abroad
  • Kennel/cattery fees
  • Unused:

    • Travel & accommodation

    • Airport parking, car hire, airport lounge pass and excursions

    • Visas/ travel documents

Once they have a diagnosis, you could add all of their conditions to their policy. This will affect the amount you pay, however it will mean that you can be covered for their conditions going forward

You can claim for anything that’s covered under your policy. It’s a good idea to check your Confirmation of Cover document and your policy wording before you need to make a claim to understand what you can claim for.

It depends. When you make a claim, you might be asked to pay some of the bill.

The amount you’ll need to pay will depend on your cover limits, excess and bill share that you chose when you set up your policy.

When it comes to excesses for ongoing conditions, you’ll only be asked to contribute your excess once per policy year for that condition.

If you’re switching pet insurance with no gap in cover, then you could claim as soon as you start your policy. 

If you’re not switching, then there will be a short delay before you can claim... But you won’t have to wait too long. This means that if your pet needs help during this time - or shows symptoms during this time - you’ll need to foot the bill yourself.

Here’s when your cover will start depending on what you need to claim for if you don’t have continuous cover:

Accidental injuries - 2 days

Illnesses - 14 days. 

Missing pets - 14 days

If your claim is likely to be more than £1,000 then you’ll need to tell us as soon as possible. Otherwise, you can go straight to MyPetsure portal and submit your claim online or through your vet if they have the option.

Before you make a claim, here’s a few things to think about to make sure your claim journey is smooth:

  1. Check your Confirmation of Cover document and policy wording to see whether you’re covered.
  2. If you’re submitting your claim yourself, keep all receipts, invoices, reports and documentation - original documents will be needed to support your claim.

Many Vets use an online system that means they can submit claims on your behalf. If your vet does this then they will submit the claim form for you. That means we can pay them directly and all you would have to pay your vet would be your excess and/or bill share amount.

Your pet’s premium will increase as they get older. 

Just like humans, as your pet gets older, there’s an increased risk of them developing new conditions and more of a chance that you’ll need to make a claim. After all, that’s why you’re paying for pet insurance. But unlike some insurers, we’ll offer you a fair price by considering your pets health and conditions to give you a price that fits. You can update your cover at renewal as your pet gets older to suit their changing needs and your pocket.

You can find all your insurance documents when you login to MyPetsure.

Your policy will automatically be renewed with your preferred payment method at the end of your cover year. If you don’t want to automatically renew, you will need  to opt out on MyPetsure or by calling us.

We’ll send you an invitation to renew your policy at least 21 days before your renewal date. We know it’s not quite as exciting as getting a birthday party invitation but we hope you’ll enjoy being with us for another year. 

In your renewal, we’ll include your premium for the next year. If you renew on a continuous payment method, we will automatically renew your policy each year using the existing payment details we have for you. 

Please contact us before your renewal date if you wish to renew using a different payment method, if you need to update any details, or amend your cover options.You can do this on MyPetsure.

When renewing, it’s important that you check your information is accurate as otherwise we might not be able to cover you when you need us.
